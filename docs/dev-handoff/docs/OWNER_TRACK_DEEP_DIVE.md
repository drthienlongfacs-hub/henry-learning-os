# Owner / Capital Allocator / Rule-Shaper Track — Deep Specification

## Purpose

This track is for building the probability that the child can later become a **creator, owner, capital allocator, founder, policy-literate citizen, leader, or rule-shaper**. It is not a “make money at any cost” track. It must cultivate stewardship, judgment, ethical pressure tolerance, and public-minded responsibility.

## Capability 1 — Deep bilingualism: Vietnamese roots, English global reach

### Why it matters
- Vietnamese is the operating system for understanding family, culture, market, law, local relationships, and Vietnam’s future opportunities.
- English is the global interface for science, technology, finance, law, research, entrepreneurship, and international networks.
- Bilingualism should not be treated as “English replaces Vietnamese”. The product must build **dual academic literacy**.

### Research and benchmark base
- AAP multilingual guidance states parents should not stop using heritage languages and notes academic, social, and self-control advantages associated with multilingualism.
- ASHA emphasizes that children learn languages best from strong models across different settings and topics, preserving family/community connection.
- CEFR gives a widely used scale for describing language ability from A1 to C2.
- Benchmark apps: Duolingo Max for roleplay/explain-my-answer; Khanmigo for guided explanation; reading apps for literacy scaffolds.

### Product modules
1. **Vietnamese Depth Studio**
   - Reading comprehension, retelling, summarizing, literary feeling, argument writing, civic vocabulary, business vocabulary.
   - Domain vocabulary: luật, chính sách, tài chính, công nghệ, y tế, khoa học, địa chính trị.
2. **English Global Interface**
   - Listening/speaking roleplay, academic reading, STEM English, debate, essay, presentation.
3. **Bilingual Transfer Mode**
   - Child explains a concept in Vietnamese, then English, then compares nuance.
   - Example: “tài sản”, “equity”, “ownership”, “stewardship”, “liability”.
4. **Cultural Code-Switching Simulator**
   - Practice how to speak with grandparents, teacher, investor, peer, public official, foreign collaborator.

### Metrics
- `vi_reading_depth_score`
- `vi_argument_quality_score`
- `en_cefr_estimate`
- `bilingual_transfer_score`
- `domain_vocabulary_mastery`
- `oral_explanation_score_vi_en`

### Keywords for agents/content
`heritage language`, `academic bilingualism`, `CEFR`, `code-switching`, `dual-language literacy`, `Vietnamese academic vocabulary`, `oracy`, `read-aloud`, `roleplay`, `translation as thinking`, `bilingual transfer`.

## Capability 2 — Mathematics, probability, data, and AI thinking

### Why it matters
Owners and capital allocators think in rates, uncertainty, base rates, optionality, risk, expected value, compounding, and data quality. AI builders need mathematical intuition and statistical literacy, not only syntax.

### Research and benchmark base
- GAISE II provides a Pre-K–12 statistics/data science framework and emphasizes statistical literacy.
- Data Science for Everyone provides K–12 learning progressions for data literacy, data science, and AI foundations.
- NCTM emphasizes high-quality mathematics education through reasoning, problem solving, assessment, and productive mathematical discourse.
- Benchmark apps: Brilliant for interactive problem solving; Khan Academy for mastery; Desmos-style visual exploration; Code.org for AI/CS.

### Product modules
1. **Number Sense & Mental Models**
   - Quantity, estimation, ratio, proportion, fraction sense.
2. **Problem Solving Lab**
   - Polya-style: understand, plan, solve, check, generalize.
3. **Probability & Decision Lab**
   - Coin flips, dice, weather, sports, medical tests, investment scenarios.
4. **Data Detective**
   - Collect, clean, visualize, interpret, question data.
5. **AI Literacy & Model Thinking**
   - What data trains models? What is bias? What is uncertainty? Why can AI hallucinate?
6. **Capital Math**
   - Compounding, margin, unit economics, risk/reward, expected value.

### Metrics
- `math_reasoning_score`
- `concept_transfer_index`
- `probabilistic_reasoning_score`
- `data_question_quality`
- `graph_interpretation_score`
- `expected_value_accuracy`
- `model_uncertainty_explanation_score`

### Keywords
`number sense`, `estimation`, `probability`, `statistics`, `data literacy`, `expected value`, `base rate`, `Bayesian thinking`, `unit economics`, `compounding`, `uncertainty`, `AI literacy`, `model risk`, `data quality`.

## Capability 3 — History, law, policy, institutions, and geopolitics

### Why it matters
People who shape rules understand how rules are made, how institutions behave, how incentives work, how nations compete/cooperate, and how historical memory shapes markets and power.

### Research and benchmark base
- C3 Framework structures social studies around civics, economics, geography, and history, emphasizing inquiry, evidence, disciplinary concepts, and communication.
- Stanford Civic Online Reasoning trains students to evaluate online information and judge source credibility.
- iCivics benchmarks simulation-based civic learning through games and scaffolds.

### Product modules
1. **History as Systems Lab**
   - Cause/effect, incentives, institutions, technology shifts, trade routes, wars, reforms.
2. **Law & Rules Simulator**
   - Family rules -> classroom rules -> city rules -> national laws -> international agreements.
3. **Policy Sandbox**
   - Child makes a policy choice and sees stakeholder tradeoffs.
4. **Geopolitics Map Room**
   - Geography, resources, chokepoints, demographics, alliances, supply chains.
5. **Civic Online Reasoning Lab**
   - Lateral reading, source checking, claim-evidence reasoning, misinformation detection.

### Metrics
- `historical_causality_score`
- `institutional_reasoning_score`
- `stakeholder_mapping_score`
- `policy_tradeoff_score`
- `source_credibility_score`
- `geopolitical_map_reasoning_score`

### Keywords
`C3 Framework`, `civic inquiry`, `historical thinking`, `institutional analysis`, `policy tradeoffs`, `geopolitics`, `lateral reading`, `source credibility`, `stakeholder analysis`, `rules of the game`.

## Capability 4 — Finance, accounting, assets, and capital allocation

### Why it matters
A future owner must understand cash flow, balance sheet, income statement, asset vs liability, risk, diversification, taxes, incentives, and long-term compounding. This is not about speculation; it is about stewardship.

### Research and benchmark base
- OECD PISA 2022 financial literacy report examines 15-year-olds’ understanding of money matters.
- OECD/INFE youth framework is outcome-based and covers core financial competencies.
- Jump$tart/CEE standards cover earning, spending, saving, investing, credit, risk, and financial decision-making.

### Product modules
1. **Money Foundations**
   - Needs/wants, earning, saving, spending, giving.
2. **Family Micro-Economy**
   - Allowance, chores, goals, tradeoffs.
3. **Accounting for Kids**
   - Cash in/out, simple ledger, assets/liabilities, profit/loss.
4. **Capital Allocation Simulator**
   - Allocate imaginary capital among education, business, savings, donation, experiments.
5. **Business Unit Economics**
   - Lemonade stand -> online store -> subscription app.
6. **Risk & Ethics**
   - Fraud, bubbles, leverage, conflicts of interest, consumer protection.

### Metrics
- `financial_concept_mastery`
- `cashflow_tracking_accuracy`
- `asset_liability_classification`
- `risk_reward_reasoning_score`
- `capital_allocation_rationale_score`
- `financial_ethics_scenario_score`

### Keywords
`financial literacy`, `accounting literacy`, `assets`, `liabilities`, `cash flow`, `income statement`, `balance sheet`, `compounding`, `risk`, `diversification`, `unit economics`, `capital allocation`, `stewardship`.

## Capability 5 — Sales, persuasion, negotiation, leadership, and coalition-building

### Why it matters
Founders, leaders, and policymakers must communicate value, recruit allies, negotiate tradeoffs, manage conflict, sell ethically, and build trust. Persuasion without ethics becomes manipulation; leadership without self-mastery becomes dominance.

### Research and benchmark base
- EntreComp defines entrepreneurship as acting on opportunities and ideas to create value for others.
- Harvard Program on Negotiation’s TNRC uses role-play simulations and teaching exercises for negotiation.
- WEF highlights leadership and social influence as rising core skills.
- Speech/debate and civics simulations demonstrate the power of structured oral reasoning and role-play.

### Product modules
1. **Pitch Lab**
   - Explain a product, idea, book, or project in 30 seconds, 2 minutes, 5 minutes.
2. **Negotiation Simulator**
   - Parent-child chores, peer project, customer complaint, investor pitch, policy compromise.
3. **Leadership Quest**
   - Plan, assign roles, give feedback, resolve conflict, debrief.
4. **Ethical Persuasion Coach**
   - Difference between explaining, persuading, nudging, manipulating, coercing.
5. **Founder Studio**
   - Identify problem, user, value proposition, prototype, feedback, iteration.

### Metrics
- `clarity_score`
- `audience_adaptation_score`
- `evidence_use_score`
- `negotiation_interest_mapping`
- `ethical_persuasion_score`
- `leadership_reflection_score`
- `coalition_building_score`

### Keywords
`oracy`, `pitch`, `storytelling`, `value proposition`, `negotiation`, `BATNA`, `interests vs positions`, `ethical persuasion`, `leadership`, `coalition`, `feedback`, `teamwork`, `entrepreneurial competence`.

## Capability 6 — Ethics, self-mastery, pressure tolerance, and character

### Why it matters
Money, power, visibility, AI, and leadership amplify character. The app must develop internal brakes: honesty, humility, courage, compassion, self-regulation, and the ability to withstand pressure without becoming cruel or corrupt.

### Research and benchmark base
- CASEL SEL framework covers self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making.
- Harvard Center on the Developing Child provides age-based executive-function and self-regulation activities.
- OECD Learning Compass emphasizes student agency, taking responsibility, creating new value, and reconciling tensions/dilemmas.
- UNICEF child-centered AI guidance stresses protection, provision, and participation.

### Product modules
1. **Ethics Under Pressure**
   - Scenarios: cheating with AI, lying for benefit, excluding a peer, using money unfairly.
2. **Executive Function Gym**
   - Working memory, inhibition, planning, flexible thinking, reflection.
3. **Resilience & Recovery Coach**
   - How to handle failure, rejection, loss, criticism, unfairness.
4. **Digital Integrity Lab**
   - AI cheating, plagiarism, privacy, cyberbullying, deepfake awareness.
5. **Responsibility Ladder**
   - Family chores, promises, commitments, service.

### Metrics
- `self_regulation_score`
- `reflection_depth_score`
- `ethical_scenario_reasoning`
- `failure_recovery_time`
- `integrity_event_resolution`
- `responsibility_completion_quality`

### Keywords
`SEL`, `executive function`, `self-regulation`, `character`, `integrity`, `ethical reasoning`, `responsibility`, `agency`, `resilience`, `pressure tolerance`, `digital citizenship`, `AI integrity`.

## Track-level dashboard

The parent and child should see a simple dashboard:

- **Agency**: Can he set goals, act, and reflect?
- **Ownership**: Does he take responsibility for learning and commitments?
- **Judgment**: Does he reason under uncertainty and tradeoffs?
- **Communication**: Can he explain, persuade, negotiate, and listen?
- **Capital sense**: Does he understand resources, time, money, and opportunity cost?
- **Ethics under pressure**: Does he stay honest and humane when incentives are high?

## Critical anti-patterns

- Teaching wealth without ethics.
- Teaching debate without listening.
- Teaching finance without stewardship.
- Teaching acceleration without emotional maturity.
- Teaching politics as tribal identity instead of institutional reasoning.
- Teaching AI as shortcut instead of amplifier of thought.
