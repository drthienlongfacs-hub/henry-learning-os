# Benchmark Matrix

## Khan Academy / Khanmigo

What to learn:
- Mastery-based structure.
- AI tutor and teacher assistant model.
- Hints and explanations.

What to avoid:
- Generic learning path not fully customized to family context.

## Duolingo / Duolingo Max

What to learn:
- Short lessons.
- Roleplay.
- Explain mistakes.
- Gamified habit loop.

What to avoid:
- Streak obsession.
- Learning points replacing real fluency.

## Anki / Quizlet

What to learn:
- Spaced repetition.
- Flashcards.
- Active recall.
- Quiz generation.

What to avoid:
- Rote memorization without understanding.

## Brilliant

What to learn:
- Interactive problem solving.
- Learn-by-doing.
- Conceptual math/science.

What to avoid:
- Too text-heavy or abstract for younger kids.

## Prodigy

What to learn:
- Adaptive practice.
- Parent/teacher dashboards.
- Game motivation.

What to avoid:
- Game loop overpowering learning.

## Scratch / ScratchJr

What to learn:
- Creative coding.
- Stories, games, animations.
- Child agency.

What to avoid:
- Uncontrolled public community for younger kids.

## Code.org

What to learn:
- K-12 computer science pathways.
- AI literacy curriculum.
- Hands-on projects.

What to avoid:
- One-size-fits-all school framing.

## Differentiation for Long Learning OS

1. Vietnamese-first but global-ready.
2. Designed for one child/family, not classroom scale first.
3. AI has multiple roles and guardrails.
4. Parent co-learning is native.
5. Whole-child development, not only academic metrics.
6. Longitudinal 12-year portfolio.
